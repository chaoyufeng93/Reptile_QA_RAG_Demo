Ackie Monitor Lizard Care Sheet 
Species Name:  
Varanus acanthurus 
Adult Size:  
60cm+ 
Lifespan:  
15-20 years 
Breeding Age:  
6-18 months  
 
Ackie monitors, scientifically known as Varanus acanthurus, are 
captivating reptiles native to northern Australia. Characterized by their 
vibrant colours and inquisitive nature, these monitor lizards are 
popular among reptile enthusiasts. Their compact size, reaching up to 
24 inches in length, makes them suitable for captive habitats. Ackies 
thrive in arid environments and possess keen hunting skills, primarily 
preying on insects, small mammals, and even other reptiles. They 
exhibit complex behaviours, including digging burrows for shelter and 
basking in the sun. Due to their intelligence and relatively manageable 
care requirements, ackie monitors have gained popularity as pets 
among reptile enthusiasts worldwide.

Housing: 
Enclosure: Provide a spacious enclosure, ideally a wooden 4ft enclosure for a 
juvenile. As your reptile grows it is likely to need an enclosure in the realms of 5-
6ft.   
Substrate: Dry substrates such as sand can be used to mimic their natural 
environment, always use reptile safe sand, ingestion of sand can cause impaction, 
most reptile sands are designed to be digestible in small amounts.  
Alternatively, a soil sand mix can be used to retain moisture to simulate the humid 
mornings in Australia.  
 
Temperature: 
Maintain a basking spot temperature of 35-40°C. 
Provide a gradient, with the cooler side around 25-35°C. 
Use a heat source like a basking light and a heat mat for heating. 
Night time temperatures can drop to 23°C. 
 
Lighting: 
Provide a UVB light source (12%) for at least 10-12 hours a day to support calcium 
metabolism and overall health. 
 
Diet: 
Feeding: Offer a varied diet of insects 
(crickets, roaches, mealworms). Ackie 
monitors can also eat rodents when they 
reach a reasonable size. 
Supplements: Dust insects with calcium and 
vitamin supplements 2-3 times a week.

Humidity and Hydration:  
A humidity of 30-50% should be maintained. 
Provide a dish for drinking and bathing. Mist enclosure occasionally to maintain 
humidity.  
 
Enrichment and Handling: 
Place hiding spots, rocks, and branches for climbing to create a stimulating 
environment. 
Provide a secure hide for them to retreat and feel safe. 
Handle and interact with your monitor gently and regularly to foster trust, but be 
mindful of their stress levels. 
 
Health Considerations: 
Regularly monitor for signs of illness, such as lethargy, changes in appetite, or 
difficulty shedding. 
Quarantine new reptiles before introducing them to existing enclosures. 
 
Remember that the care requirements for Ackie Monitors may vary slightly based 
on individual needs, so observe your reptile’s behaviour and make adjustments 
accordingly. Always prioritize the well-being and health of your pet by providing a 
well-maintained and enriched environment.
